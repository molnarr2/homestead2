## How long are goats pregnant?

A goat's gestation lasts about **150 days** — roughly five months. Most does
kid between day 145 and day 155, so it pays to start watching a few days before
the calculated due date.

## Counting from the breeding date

The simplest way to predict kidding is to add 150 days to the date the doe was
covered. If you know the exact breeding date, your estimate will be within a few
days. If you only know a date range, use the earliest cover date so you start
watching early.

## Signs kidding is close

- Ligaments beside the tail head soften and seem to "disappear."
- The udder fills and tightens (bagging up).
- The doe separates from the herd and may paw at bedding.

## Be ready in the watch window

Have a clean, draft-free pen, towels, iodine for navels, and your vet's number
handy. Most does kid without help — your job is mostly to watch and stay calm.

The app keeps every doe's due date in front of you and reminds you as kidding
approaches, so a surprise in the back field never catches you off guard.
