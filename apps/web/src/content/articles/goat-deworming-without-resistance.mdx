## Why calendar deworming backfires

Deworming every animal on a fixed schedule is the fastest way to breed
**resistant parasites**. The worms that survive each treatment are the ones that
pass on resistance — and eventually the dewormer stops working at all.

## Deworm by need, not by date

Modern guidance is to treat **selectively**, using tools like FAMACHA scoring
(checking eyelid color for anemia) and occasional fecal egg counts to find the
animals that actually need it. Often only a fraction of the herd does.

## A sensible starting cadence

A generated schedule is still useful as a **reminder to check** — not a command
to treat. Our
[deworming & vaccination schedule generator](/tools/deworming-vaccination-schedule)
builds a dated plan you can adjust with your vet, and you can export it to your
calendar.

## Pair it with vaccinations

Core vaccines like CDT are genuinely calendar-based and easy to forget. Keeping
them on the same schedule as your parasite checks means one routine to remember —
and the app turns that routine into reminders that re-create themselves each
season.
