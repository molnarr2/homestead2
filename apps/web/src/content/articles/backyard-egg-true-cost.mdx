## Are backyard eggs really cheaper?

It depends almost entirely on **feed cost** and **how well your hens lay**. A
flock in full lay through summer can easily beat grocery prices; the same flock
in a winter molt can cost more per dozen than the store.

## The simple math

1. Work out your feed cost per pound (bag price ÷ bag weight).
2. Multiply by how much your flock eats per month.
3. Divide total monthly cost by the dozens your hens actually lay.

That last number is your **cost per dozen**. Our
[egg & feed cost calculator](/tools/chicken-egg-feed-cost-calculator) does all of
this in a few seconds.

## Levers that move the number

- **Feed price:** buying in bulk or splitting pallets with neighbors helps most.
- **Lay rate:** light in winter and good nutrition keep hens producing.
- **Waste:** treadle feeders and dry storage cut spillage and spoilage.

## Know your real numbers

Estimates are a starting point. Logging actual egg counts and feed buys over a
season turns guesses into your true cost — which is exactly what the app's
production tracking is for.
