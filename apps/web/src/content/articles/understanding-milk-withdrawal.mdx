## What "withdrawal" actually means

When you treat an animal with a medication, residues stay in the milk, meat, or
eggs for a period of time. The **withdrawal period** is how long you must wait
after the last dose before that product is safe to sell or eat.

## Milk and meat can differ

The same drug often has a **different** withdrawal time for milk than for meat.
Penicillin, for example, commonly clears milk in a few days but requires a much
longer wait before slaughter. Always read both numbers off the label.

## Why the label is the authority

Withdrawal times change with the brand, the dose, the route (injection vs. oral),
the species, and the country you're in. Any chart online — including our
[withdrawal calculator](/tools/medication-withdrawal-calculator) — is a planning
convenience only. **Confirm every withdrawal against the product label and your
veterinarian** before anything leaves the farm.

## Track it so you never sell early

Mark the safe date the moment you treat an animal. The app does this for you:
it records every treatment and warns you while milk, meat, or eggs are still in
their withdrawal window.
